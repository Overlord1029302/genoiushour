Aloe Vera

Scientific Name: Asphodelaceae

Family: Asphodelaceae

Native Range: Primarily found in the Arabian Peninsula but cultivated worldwide.

Description: A succulent plant with thick, fleshy leaves that contain a gel renowned for its soothing properties, often used in skin care and traditional medicine.

Care Tips: Thrives in bright, indirect sunlight and needs very little water, allowing the soil to dry out between waterings.

Interesting Fact: Aloe Vera has been used for centuries in traditional medicine, primarily for its skin-soothing properties.
